Beliefs Changes: Step 8 -> Step 9 (major restructuring)
--------------------------------------------------------
The entire level_strategy was reorganized from a "Barrier Bypass Protocol" focus
to a "Self-Preservation & Barrier Bypass Protocol" with a new priority hierarchy:

World Knowledge additions:
  1. Multi-step action understanding: eat, zap, apply, kick require multiple
     inputs. Game awaits follow-up input (item selection, direction) before
     the turn ends.
  2. "Retry" message reinterpretation: "Your previous output did not contain a
     valid action. Retry" can mean the game is awaiting follow-up input for a
     multi-step action, not just bad syntax.
  3. Satiation/choking risk: eating when "Satiated" risks choking death.
  4. Power/charge tracking: Pw:X(Y) indicates wand charges (X current, Y max).
  5. Cursor position as ground truth: cursor: (x, y) interpreted as (y, x) is
     the agent's absolute position.

Level Strategy restructuring:
  1. Added "Immediate Threat Triage" as #1 priority with 3 sub-rules:
     - Heal at <50% HP (supersedes all other actions)
     - Escape/neutralize adjacent monster before multi-turn healing at <25% HP
     - Complete multi-step actions once initiated (e.g., provide item letter
       for eat)
  2. Added "Specific Hazard Interaction" to Barrier Bypass Protocol: if
     continuous lava and wand of cold in inventory, prioritize zapping.
  3. Added far movement caveat: not guaranteed disengagement, re-evaluate if
     still in combat.
  4. Added "Monster Engagement/Avoidance" as explicit strategy point #4.
  5. Added "Loop Detection & Escape Strategy": detect repetitive exploration,
     trigger backtracking to unexplored intersections.
  6. Added "Burden Management": drop non-essential items like corpses when
     Burdened.

Beliefs Changes: Step 9 -> Step 10 (surgical refinements)
----------------------------------------------------------
Only small incremental changes:

World Knowledge additions:
  1. Zapping wand of cold at lava is a "proven way" to bypass (upgraded from
     implicit). Added: "Unidentified wands reveal their true nature (e.g.,
     wand of cold) upon successful first use."
  2. Added wield and fire to multi-step action list.
  3. Added: "Moving into a tile occupied by a Monster initiates combat. This
     can be used as an attack action."
  4. Added: "There is nothing here to pick up" explanation (auto-pickup or
     transient visual effect).

Level Strategy refinements:
  1. Wand of cold strategy now says "identified or not" and includes explicit
     prompt-response instructions (select unknown wand, choose direction).
  2. Monster engagement updated to: "consider fighting the monster by moving
     into its square as an implicit attack."

   - Agents became trapped in confined areas bounded by continuous molten lava
   - They repeatedly traced the same perimeter without finding exits
   - Several had wand of cold in inventory but never tried using it
   - No mechanism existed to detect being stuck in a loop

   - Agent correctly identified lava barrier, exhausted search/movement options
   - Then experimentally zapped a "forked wand" at the lava
   - This revealed it was a wand of cold, solidified the lava, killed a lichen,

================================================================================
PART 4: WHY THIS SEQUENCE CAUSED THE PERFORMANCE JUMP
================================================================================

The critical insight is the TWO-STEP LEARNING DYNAMIC:

Step 9 provided the structural framework (survive first, understand multi-step
actions, detect loops) but was learned from mostly-failing rollouts (1/10
success). The beliefs were correct but the agent still lacked confident
operational knowledge about HOW to actually solve the level. Performance
actually dropped (0.25 -> 0.05).

Step 10 provided confident operational details learned from 10/10 successes.
The key changes were small but decisive:

  - Telling the agent to zap UNIDENTIFIED wands (not just "wand of cold") meant
    it would actually attempt the lava bypass in practice
  - Explicit combat-via-movement meant it could handle monsters instead of
    getting stuck avoiding them
  - "Proven way" language removed hesitation

BOOTSTRAP EFFECT:
  The step 9 beliefs set up the right priorities so the rollouts in step 10
  could succeed (the structural improvements -- threat triage, loop detection,
  multi-step action understanding -- enabled the agent to survive long enough
  to discover the lava bypass). The step 10 refinements then locked in the
  confident, actionable knowledge from those successes.

  Step 9 (structural) -> enabled successful step 10 rollouts -> step 10
  (operational) -> locked in reliable performance

  The structural improvements alone (step 9) weren't enough without the
  concrete operational knowledge (step 10). And the operational knowledge
  couldn't have been discovered without the structural framework enabling
  the agent to survive and explore effectively.

This is a clear example of how the explore/improve loop bootstraps itself:
failure teaches the agent how to survive, survival enables discovery, and
discovery teaches the agent how to succeed.
