## HOW BELIEFS ARE LEARNED: THE MECHANISM
================================================================================

The explore loop works as follows:
1. Each step runs 10 experiments (single-episode rollouts with specific goals)
2. The LLM summarizes what was learned from each experiment's trajectory
3. The improve step integrates these summaries to update beliefs and perception

Three learning mechanisms observed:

### 1. Warm Prior (Steps 1-2)
Some knowledge (lava=hazard, wand of cold exists, stairs, etc.) was present
from the warm start. This provides a foundation but isn't sufficient for
reliable gameplay.

### 2. Failure-Driven Refinement (Steps 3-9)
Most learning comes from analyzing WHY experiments fail:
  - Agent walks into lava → strengthen hazard avoidance beliefs
  - Agent can't navigate → add Movement Outcomes to perception (step 4)
  - Agent stuck at lava barrier → experiment with items → discover zap works (step 5)
  - Agent gets stuck in loops → add loop detection (step 9)

### 3. Success Consolidation (Steps 10+)
Once a critical mass of correct beliefs exists:
  - Successful experiments CONFIRM beliefs (potential → proven)
  - The system generates tightly focused experiments that test variations
  - Edge cases are discovered (self-zap, door vs monster disambiguation)

The step 10 breakthrough happened because:
  (a) Perception had Movement Outcomes (since step 4)
  (b) Beliefs had the freeze-lava strategy (since step 5)
  (c) Multi-step action handling was adequate (since step 8)
  (d) The system had enough successful examples to upgrade strategy confidence

Summary of 24 failures across 120 eval runs (steps 15-20):
  - Walked into lava:     7 (29%) — LLM ignores hazard warnings
  - Timeout/stuck:        7 (29%) — agent wanders without finding solution
  - Self-zap bolt of cold: 4 (17%) — zaps wand in wrong direction
  - Killed by monsters:    4 (17%) — fails to heal/flee
  - Killed by holy water:  2 ( 8%) — unexpected game mechanic

~75% of failures relate to lava management (lava death + self-zap + timeout
from inability to cross). The remaining failures are LLM stochasticity
(ignoring beliefs) and edge-case game mechanics.

Contributing factors:
  1. Temperature=1.0 — high stochasticity in LLM outputs
  2. 100-step limit — tight for harder random maps
  3. 20 different seeds per eval step — map difficulty varies
  4. Beliefs are advisory, not enforced — LLM can and does ignore them
